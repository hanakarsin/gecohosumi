Reprieve as loan defaulters set to get 30-day notice before CRB listing

Loan defaulters will be notified within 30 days before their names are forwarded to credit reference bureaus (CRBs) for blacklisting if a proposed bill is adopted.

The new bill contained in the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) regulations 2019 would protect borrowers against financial lending institutions while at the same time offer a level playground.

Should it pass, such institutions will be compelled to inform defaulters through writing of their intention to report them to CRB for listing, Business Daily reported on Thursday, June 5.

“An institution and a third party credit information provider shall notify the customer within one month before a loan becomes non-performing that the institution shall submit to a bureau the information on the loan immediately it becomes non-performing,” CBK proposes.

Following mushrooming of mobile loan applications, there have been cases of Kenyans either finding their names on the bad CRB book or receiving calls from the loaners demanding payment which they know nothing about.

Once listed, the defaulters will not be able to access loan facilities with commercial banks, Saccos or a majority of the mobile money lenders.

The CBK Governor Patrick Njoroge recently called for proper regulations to curb rise of mobile money apps in the same way banks operate particularly on customers' data privacy.

“There has to be a proper regulation, where similar products are regulated in a similar way so long as you are lending to customers or receiving deposits. If you have a banking function, it’s not just about the name; you have to be regulated in the same way or it will lead to arbitrage,” said Njoroge.

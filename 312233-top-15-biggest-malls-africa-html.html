Top 15 biggest malls in Africa

There are several biggest malls in Africa you would admire to visit or set up your business in. You can locate these malls across Africa with each occupying a large piece of land.

There is one thing that you will always love about malls. It is a place you will walk into and find everything under one roof. A mall has you covered if you want to shop for household stuff, have your aching tooth checked and have lunch while your car gets cleaned. One fundamental feature that makes Mall luring is their sizes.

Biggest malls in Africa

About two decades ago, Africa appeared as the most underdeveloped continent. However, in recent years, Africa has taken significant strides in its development initiatives which have seen the largest shopping malls build on its soil. Some of these malls stand out thanks to their magnificence and luring business ambient.

1. Morocco Mall

Morocco Resort is one of the biggest shopping malls in Africa. It is located on the west coast of Morocco in Casablanca. The Mall offers its visitors the rare opportunity of going for scuba diving in the Center's million-liter aquarium.

2. Two Rivers Mall

Two Rivers is the biggest Mall in East Africa. The Mall is partly owned by Centum and majorly by the Kenya billionaire Dr. Chris Kirubi. It is located in Nairobi, Kenya on an area of 65,000 square meters. The shopping Center hosts numerous international and local retailers.

3. Mall of Africa

It is located in the Waterfall City, Midrand, Gauteng. The shopping destination is owned by Atterbury property group. It occupies an area of about 13000 square meters and boasts of 6500 parking spaces. The Architectural design of this particular Mall is otherworldly. It depicts geology in Africa, mainly the oil minerals, rainforest, deserts and lakes.

4. The Mall of Arabia

It is the largest and thus ranked top on the list of shopping malls in Africa. The Mall is located in Cairo, Egypt, on an area of approximately 267,000 square meters. It is owned by a Saudi Organization known as Fawaz Al Hokair. The Center houses modern cinema complexes, international gyms, luxurious hotels plus shops with global products.

5. Cairo Festival city

It is located in Cairo festival city, occupying 160,000 feet squared. Cairo festival houses American international school, big office spaces, fabulous villas, internationals hotels and motor vehicle assembly units. Also available are eateries and hypermarkets.

6. Sandton City

Sandton city is not only one of the biggest malls in Africa but also among the oldest shopping Centers on the continent. It opened doors to its customers way back in 1973. The place is owned by Pareto Limited and Liberty Holdings Limited and occupies an area of about 128 square feet. It has virtually everything that a customer could desire.

7. The Gateway Theatre of Shopping

This is a prestigious shopping Center that was built by Old Mutual properties between the years 1998 and 2001. The Gateway Theatre of Shopping is situated in Umhlanga, Durban in South Africa. It is perhaps the biggest Mall in South Africa since it occupies an area of 220,000 square meters with 12,000 parking spaces.

8. Canal Walk Mall

Canal Walk never misses on the list of shopping malls in South Africa. It was opened in 2000 and located in Cape Town with an affluent surrounding. Situated on 141,000 square feet, it boasts of over 400 stores with luxurious products. What is spectacular about the place is the East and West towers that provide moderns office space. Visitors get a chance to watch the famous Robben Island and the Table Mountains.

9. Dembel City Center

Dembel City Center is one of the biggest Ethiopia shopping malls. It is located in the center of Addis Ababa. The twelve floors structure was built in 2002, and it has 123 spaces designated for use as shops or offices. It was the first western-style shopping center to be opened in Ethiopia.

10. Novare Lekki Mall

Novare Lekki Mall is the biggest in Lagos, Nigeria. It opened to the public in August 2016 thanks to the rapidly urbanizing district of Lekki. The place is owned by Novare Real Estate Africa and occupies an area of 22,000 square meters. Novare Lekki mall houses signature stores such as Shoprite, Nike, Levi’s, Game, and Swatch.

11. Game City Mall

This is the most significant and busiest shopping Center in Botswana’s capital city, Gaborone. It has been functional since 2003 and covers and a space of about 50,000 square meters. If you are looking for fashion stores, specialty stores, and restaurants, this is the place to go to in Botswana. The cloth retailers here are Nike, Levi’s, and Edgars. On the other hand, the restaurants at the site are, Nandos, Chicken Licken and KFC franchises.

12. West Hills Mall

West Hills Mall is one of the largest shopping Malls in West Africa. It is situated in Ghana about a 30 minutes’ drive from Accra. The place covers an area of about 93,000 square meters and has a blend of South African, local Ghanaian and international brand. It has stores such as Edgar, Palace, and Shoprite. The Mall was opened in 2014, and it is owned by National Insurance Trust, Delico Property Investments and Social Security.

13. Le Caudan Waterfront

Le Caudan Waterfront is located in the Mauritius capital. It’s a top business and refreshment center in the island nation. The Mall is adjacent to luxurious business and travel hotels and therefore making it convenient for the visitors to enjoy shopping and the capital city.

14. Cresta Shopping Center

Cresta is one of the largest shopping malls in South Africa. The Mall boasts of about 260 stores and quality services. It is located in Johannesburg on 104,143 square meters.

15. Tinapa Shopping resort

Tinapa Shopping Mall is the largest shopping resort in Nigeria. It covers a total area of about 861,000 square meters. It is located in the Tinapa Free Zone close to Calabar, and it belongs to Tinapa Business Resort Limited. The resort has a parking capacity that can accommodate almost 4000 cars.

These are some of the biggest malls in Africa. However, the list of shopping malls is long. These are the exciting place you can drop by to shop or enjoy other services.

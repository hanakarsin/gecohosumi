KDF officer becomes first Kenyan to get Hodsons Merit Award at British military academy

A Kenyan military officer has received the overall Hodson's Merit Award for displaying impeccable and exemplary performance during training at the Royal Military Academy in Britain.

Stephen Githongo Keiya became the first Kenyan to receive the global prestigious military award after years of participation in the high-end training.

Keiya was received by commander of Kenya army Lieutenant General Raria Koipaton at the Kenya army headquarters on Friday, August 23.

General Koipaton hailed the KDF officer for the accomplishment and endeared him to continue to uphold the training values so as to become an officer of character and great hounour in the military.

"The tenets of leadership and command should always be your guide as you take up your new role," he told the officer.

Keiya was honoured alongside another Kenyan Cadet, Sally Jeruto Chebii, who equally showcased exemplary performance skills during the training.

"The officers performed exemplary well with Lt Keiya receiving the Hodson’s Merit Award for the best overall performance by an international cadet, becoming the first recipient of the award from Kenya," KDF stated on its official Facebook page

The winner was honoured on Friday, August 9, during the Sovereign’s Commissioning Parade at the Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst.

The Kenyan officers were among 33 international cadets out of 243 who underwent a rigorous training programme that involved basic military skills and leadership guidance.

The two officers are alumni of Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology.

Keiya and Chebii hold Bachelor of Science degrees in Disaster Management and International Diplomacy and Civil and Structural Engineering respectively.

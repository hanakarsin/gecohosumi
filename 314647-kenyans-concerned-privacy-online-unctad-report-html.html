Kenyans least concerned about their privacy online - UNCTAD report

- The survey found only 44% of Kenyans online cared about security of their personal data

- Highest level of concern was noted in Egypt, Hong Kong, India, Mexico and Nigeria

- Report indicated although there appears to be increasing concern about data privacy, users continue to give away personal data

Kenyans are the most careless people when it comes to safeguarding their personal data and privacy on the internet, a new survey has revealed.

The global survey conducted by Centre for International Governance Innovation and Ipsos in collaboration with United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) and Internet Society found only 44% of internet users in Kenya were concerned about their privacy online.

The report seen by TUKO.co.ke on Thursday, September 5, showed some 78% internet users in 25 countries were at least somewhat concerned about their privacy online.

"Concerns were found to be widespread in Egypt, Hong Kong in China, India, Mexico and Nigeria, where the proportion was found to be 90% or higher. By contrast, the lowest level of concern was noted in Kenya at 44%," the report indicated.

The survey showed although there seems to be increasing concern about data privacy and online security around the world, there is somewhat a "data privacy paradox" as users continue to give away personal data and consequently their privacy in exchange for different services.

"Many of these service are offered by various platforms free of charge or on a take-it-or-leave-it basis. The situation has been described as someone who is not paying for product becomes the product," the report said.

The researchers found social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter were the second biggest sources of distrust on the internet after cyber criminals.

The survey was conducted between December 21, 2018, and February 10, 2019 during which 25,229 internet users in a select 25 countries were interviewed.

The report comes amid rising concern over cyber crimes in Kenya, with a recent report released by the Communications Authority of Kenya (CA) in July 2019 indicating Kenyan firms were hit by an estimated 11.2 million cyber threats between January and March 2019, a 10.1% rise compared to 2018.

A related report by cyber security firm Serianu revealed the country lost to a staggering KSh 2.95 billion to online based fraudsters in 2018 alone, up from KSh 2.1 billion recorded in 2017.

Serianu Chief Operating Officer Joseph Mathenge noted the cons were mostly targeting financial institutions with banks and Sacco's being among the hardest hit.

List of countries with monarchy governments in 2020

A monarchy is a type of government where there is a single ruler that supervises the events, decisions, and success of a given state. Even though most countries in the 21st century are democratic when it comes to electing their leaders, there are still some countries with monarchy governments. The monarch inherits the leadership position and rules over the country until their death or abdication.

Presently, 44 sovereign countries across the globe have a monarch as their head of state. Out of these states, 10 are in North America, 13 in Asia, 3 in Africa, 12 in Europe, and 6 in Oceania. Below we take a look at some reigning monarchs in the world.

Types of monarchy

Different states across the globe have distinct characteristics distinguishing them. Not all monarchs have autocratic powers in the countries in which they head. For some, the rulers are purely ceremonial and considered as only figureheads. In some exceptional circumstances, a regent becomes the ruler if the monarch is an infant, unavailable, or incompetent of the ruling.

A coregency is a unique monarchy where two sovereigns are ruling at the same time. Also, monarch titles differ from one sovereignty to the other. There are five standard titles used by reigning monarchs across the world. These are King, Sultan, President of the Pontification Commission, Minister of State, and Prime Minister.

2. Bahrain

Bahrain is an island county situated within the Persian Gulf. Bahrain Domain is one of the constitutional monarchy countries. It is ruled by King Sheikh Hamad Bin Isa Al Khalifa who took over leadership in 1999.

3. The Kingdom of Belgium

The Belgium realm is sited in Northwestern Europe. Their monarch is titled as King, currently Phillipe or Filip. Even though Belgium has a constitutional monarchy, King Phillipe, who began his reign in 2013 is a ceremonial head of state.

4. Brunei Darussalam

Brunei, a small nation found in the Southeast Asian island of Borneo, is another monarch country. It is governed by Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah who rose to power in 1967. Besides this, the Sultan also became the nation’s prime minister when it attained its independence in 1984.

5. Eswatini

Eswatini also referred to as Swaziland is a small landlocked county found in the Southern part of Africa. The Kingdom of Swaziland is an absolute diarchy becoming one of the monarchy states with queens and kings in the world. Their King is known as “Ngwenyama,” and their queen is “Ndlovukati. King Mswati III began his rule in 1986, and Ntfombi is his queen.

6. Japan

Japan is one of the constitutional monarchy countries with a ruler who is the 125th member of the world’s most ancient dynasty. The country’s monarch Emperor Akihito sat on the throne in 1989. On April 30th, 2019, Emperor Akihito abdicated himself paving the way for his son Naruhito to be the new Emperor of the Chrysanthemum Throne. Ruler Akihito became the first Japanese monarch to resign since 1817.

7. The Kingdom of Lesotho

Lesotho is one of the three monarchy nations in Africa. It is a tiny country enclaved within South Africa. Their King, Letsie III has been the ruler of the Kingdom since 1996. He holds a ceremonial role.

8. Malaysia

This list of kingdoms in the world would not be complete without mentioning Malaysia, a country with a unique rotational monarchy system. Malaysia crowned its 16th king in January 2019, Sultan Abdullah Sultan Ahmad Shah. In this country’s constitutional monarchy nine ethnic Malay state sovereigns take turns to rule over the nation on a five-year term basis.

9. Saudi Arabia

The Saudi Arabia Empire can be found on the Arabian Peninsula in the Middle East. This kingdom is one of the countries with absolute monarchy. King Salman bin Abdulaziz al-Saud is the nation’s acting Prime Minister as well as Head of State since 2015.

10.Vatican City

Vatican City, situated in Rome, is the smallest independent state in the world. The country’s regime is an absolute elective monarchy governed by the Roman Catholic Church. Pope Francis, who was elected in 2013, is a non-hereditary monarch also known as a religious monarch.

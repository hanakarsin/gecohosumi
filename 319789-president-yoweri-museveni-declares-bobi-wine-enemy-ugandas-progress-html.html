President Yoweri Museveni declares Bobi Wine enemy of Uganda's progress

- Museveni said his administration was not repressive but was only protecting Ugandans from their enemy

- The long-serving president accused Wine of inciting foreigners against investing in the country

- The National Resistance Movement (NRM) leader said he will only relinquish power if asked to do so by his party

- Museveni and the MP are expected to face off in the upcoming presidential election slated for 2021

President Yoweri Museveni of Uganda has declared his fiercest critic, Bobi Wine, as the country's enemy of progress.

Museveni, who has been at the helm of Uganda's leadership for 33 years, said the Kyadondo East MP had no interest of Ugandans at heart and authorities will continue to deal with him as an enemy.

The president who was speaking to BBC refuted claims that his administration was being repressive and accused Wine of trying to undermine his leadership by inciting foreigners against investing in Uganda.

"Bobi Wine went to America and said that people should not come invest in Uganda. That means he is an enemy of progress in Uganda. When you go and tell foreigners that they should not come and invest in our country, you are waging war on our prosperity," Museveni told BBC.

He partially admitted the opposition MP's "political highhandedness" in the country had led to the cancellation of most of his music concerts and subsequent arrests.

Wine, a musician-turned-politician, has suffered political harassment, arrest and persecution ever since he joined politics in 2017 following a parliamentary by-election in Kyadondo which he won.

He has remained at loggerheads with the Ugandan leader whom he has constantly been accusing of presiding over an oppressive regime, accusations Museveni flatly dismissed saying he was only protecting Ugandans from their enemy.

"We are not here for a show, we are not theatre goers, we are people who are here to deal with very big issues of Uganda and Africa," said the NRM leader.

While Wine has vowed to bring to an end the reign of Museveni, the president was categorical that he still has unfinished business and will only relinquish power if his party asks him to do so.

The 75-year-old leader will be seeking to defend his seat for the sixth term in an election slated for 2021.

The Kyadondo East MP who is leading The People Power movement has emerged as his main opponent in the upcoming election which Museveni is determined to win again.

In so doing, the NRM leader has been deploying state machinery to scuttle the opposition's political advancements in the country and detaining his critics without trial.

He recently banned Ugandans from putting on red berets which are Wine's signature headgear.

Museveni also threatened to close five media houses which were reportedly covering and airing political activities by the People Power leader.

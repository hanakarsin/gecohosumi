Nairobi Muslim Academy fees structure, location, contacts

The right foundation from the start is crucial in molding a child’s character and personality. For this reason, it is crucial to find an institution that impacts positive attitudes and skills. Nairobi Muslim Academy is one of the best schools that instill Islam teachings and values while at the same time enabling its students to achieve academic excellence.

Nairobi Muslim Academy is an Islamic school which aims at providing quality education that is founded on the principles of Islam. Islamic morals and student development are given great importance. Since Muslim Ummah was highly prejudiced, it left the Muslim community lagging on the necessary education. This, therefore, led the Nairobi Islamic Charitable Waqf to feel the urgency to establish an academic institution oriented on Islam.

About Nairobi Muslim Academy

We all have different preferences for our young ones when it comes to their education. For parents who would want their children to join an Islamic boarding school, then Nairobi Muslim Academy is one of the best choices. It is also one of the leading Islamic primary schools in Nairobi.

Nairobi Muslim Academy was founded by Nairobi Islamic Charitable Waqf in 1995. The aim of establishing the institution was to give different levels of education to Muslims. Below is the vision, mission and the objectives of Nairobi Muslim Academy:

Vision

To be a leading centre that provides integrated Islamic and secular education.

Mission

To provide holistic, quality education based on Islamic values and principles.

Objectives

  • To provide affordable quality integrated education to Muslim children in Kenya.
  • To operate and manage an efficient Islamic institution on a sustainable basis.
  • To facilitate da’wah & Islamic activities.
  • To provide our learners with sound character education based on Islam.
  • To mold responsible citizens with good morals.
  • To provide a good learning environment with an Islamic atmosphere.

Primary

At the primary level, the school teaches both Islamic and secular studies. Admission is done at the start of every term. For the admission, an interview examination is carried out and it is done during the holidays after schools are closed. The following are the requirements for the interview and admission.

Interview requirements

  • An interview fee of Kshs 1,000 which is non-refundable
  • A copy of the pupil’s birth certificate
  • A letter of transfer from the prior institution
  • A copy(s) of ID or Passport of guardian/parent(s)
  • A copy of report forms for two terms

Admission requirements

  • The first requirement is to pass the interview
  • Parents are required to fill out the application form
  • Pay the school fees on the reporting date
  • Have the full school uniform and the games kit
  • Have all textbooks and stationery required

Secondary

To gain admission into secondary school, the student must have attained 320 marks in the KCPE. For students that come from Northern Kenya or some parts of the Coast, the KCPE cut-off marks were reduced to 300 marks based on affirmative action. Before being admitted, the school will carry out a preliminary examination for the interviewee(s) to ensure they meet the school criteria. Nairobi Muslim academy fees structure

Here is the fee structure for the nursery, primary, and secondary sections;

Nursery

Each pupil is required to pay the following per term:

  • Baby class: Kshs 30,000 per term
  • KG2 – KG3: Kshs 32,000 per term
  • Pre- unit: Kshs 35,000 per term
  • Admission Fee: Kshs 3,000 once a year
  • Report/Diary/Exercise book: Kshs 1,000 once a year

Primary

The fees for the primary section is as follows;

  • Standard 1 – 2: Kshs 35,000
  • Standard 3 – 4: Kshs 37,000
  • Standard 5 – 6: Kshs 40,000
  • Standard 7 – 8: Kshs 45,000
  • Admission fee: Kshs 5000 once a year
  • Diary/ Report/Exercise book: Kshs 1000 once a year

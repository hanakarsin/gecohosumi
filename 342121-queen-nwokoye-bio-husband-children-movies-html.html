Queen Nwokoye bio, husband, children and movies

Who is Queen Nwokoye? Most of the movie celebrities we know are from Hollywood and the Western mainstream media and entertainment industries. However, in the last several years, other industries such as Nollywood have advanced greatly, turning their stars into household names across the world.

Queen Nwokoye is one such star of the Nollywood industry. She is a famous Nollywood (Nigerian) actress who has several film roles under her name. However, there is much more to her than just being an actress. She is also a model, entrepreneur, television personality and philanthropist.

Profile summary

  • Name: Queen Nwokoye
  • Birth Date: August 11, 1982
  • Place of Birth: Lagos State, Nigeria.
  • Education: Azikiwe University, Awka
  • Profession: Actress
  • Husband: Oluchi Uzoma

Early life

Wondering how Queen Nwokoye was like growing up? Well, Queen was born on August 11, 1982. She is currently 37 years old. But what state is Queen Nwokoye from? She was born in Lagos State, Nigeria. However, her family hails from Ihembosi, in the Ekwusigo local government region of Anambra State, Nigeria.

Nwokoye was born into a large family of seven. Her parents were devout Catholics, and thus she was brought up in a strict Catholic background. On top of that, her mother and grandparents were teachers. This led to a strict upbringing, where most of her early life revolved around school and church. As a result, she ended up being a very reserved and shy teenager.

Nonetheless, Queen has stated that it was due to this upbringing that she learned to become disciplined and determined. Besides, being born into a large family, she enjoyed the company of her siblings, especially her sisters who she would play with.

Education

Queen, being an actress is not just a pretty face. She is a very educated personality who has reached the university level. She started her elementary education at Air Force Primary School. Later on, she enrolled in Queens College in Enugu for her secondary school education, from where she obtained her West African Senior School Certificate (WAEC).

After her secondary education, Nwokoye went on to be admitted to Azikiwe University in Awka. At the end of her studies, she graduated with a degree in Sociology and Anthropology.

Career

While growing up, Queen Nwokoye had dreamt of becoming a lawyer, but life had other plans for her. While in school, she developed a passion for acting, which would later propel her into a prosperous acting career.

After completing her National Youth Service, Queen moved to Lagos where she joined the Actors Guild of Nigeria. She started going for auditions, and soon after she got her first debut in Nollywood in a film titled Nna Meen. It was this film that paved the way for a successful career spanning over 250 film roles.

Soon after her debut, she landed several others roles in the industry, and featured in films such as His Majesty (2004), Security Risk (2004), The Girl is Mine (2004), Back Drop (2005), Save the Baby (2005), Last Kobo (2006) Speak the Word (2006), My Girlfriend (2006) and Lady of Faith (2006).

International recognition

Nwokoye continued to shine in the industry, but in 2014, she would gain widespread recognition from outstanding performances in two roles. The first one was her role as Ada Mbano, in a 2014 film under the same name. The film was well-received by Nollywood fans across the world, and she received an Africa Movie Academy Awards nomination for her performance. It was one of her most challenging role yet in the industry, as she had to study and speak the Imo State dialect.

The second role that brought her to limelight was Chetanna, a 2014 hit film in which Queen was a lead actress, and which was screened in the 2014 World Igbo Festival of Arts in the U.S. It is through this movie that she gained widespread international recognition.

Since then, Queen has gone on to feature in several other films such as Blind Bartimus (2015), Agaracha (2016), and New Educated Housewife (2017). Queen’s sensational acting skills and international recognition has attracted many fans to the Nollywood industry and earned her the moniker “Darling of Nollywood” from her fans. She has credited her success and outstanding performances to her family, stating that it was their full support that paved the way to the growth of her acting skills.

Family

Queen Nwokoye siblings

Not much is known about her siblings, other than the fact that she has six siblings, three sisters, and three brothers. Due to the strict religious lifestyle of her parents, Queen was only left to interact and play with her sisters.

Queen Nwokoye husband

Is Queen Nwokoye the actress married? And if so, who is the husband of Queen Nwokoye? With how Nwokoye keeps her life private, many people don’t know whether she is married or not.

Queen Nwokoye is married to her husband, Oluchi Uzoma. The couple tied the knot in a secret ceremony, which she stated was in an effort to prevent a media circus during the wedding.

Uzoma is a successful businessman who hails from Imo State and is based in South Africa. In 2019, he joined politics, where he ran for a seat in the Imo State House of Assembly. However, he did not manage to win.

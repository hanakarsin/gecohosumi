Former Taita-Taveta Women Rep Joyce Lay becomes gospel musician

- Joyce Lay revealed to have switched to music which happened during her stay in the US

- The former politician said it took her 47 years to realise her calling which started after she formed a deep spiritual journey with God

- Lay said she was not sure of what her next step would have been and had made the decision to relocate to the US and venture into business

Former Taita Taveta Women Representative Joyce Lay has revealed to have chosen a new path after her political career came to an end.

Lay chose a musical path after losing a senatorial bid, which she said was a revelation she got while spending some time in the United States.

Sharing several messages on her Facebook page, the former politician said it took her 47 years to realise her calling which started after she formed a deep spiritual journey with God.

"In 2018 I started on a deep spiritual journey. To know God more than what a preacher said. I wanted to have a deeper relationship and a wider understanding of who God is and why He created me. I started reading His word more and spending time in prayer. God is faithful," she wrote.

In another post, Lay said she was not sure of what her next step would be and had made the decision to relocate to the US and venture into business.

"I went to the US for about four months. I had made up my mind that I was going to relocate there and venture into business. I had done my research and a business plan. In the middle of all this that looked like my own good plan, one day I was sitting outside the porch reflecting on my journey and tried to understand what was ahead of me and also trying to confirm if the decision I had made was the right one," she added.

READ ALSO: Jen Bricker Bauer: Gymnast who was born without legs but has achieved success

While all this was happening, a song came into her mind and she sang it for about 10 minutes which she described as not normal.

The former woman representative said she then decided to record it on the phone and then the studio.

Lay went on to record other songs and launched her first hit, Najua Wewe Ni Bwana, on Monday, June 29, which happened to be her birthday.

In 2019, TUKO.co.ke reported she had returned to the US to visit her father-in-law who was approaching his 99th birthday.

In a Facebook post dated Thursday, September 26, the former MP revealed her father-in-law was to celebrate his 99 years on earth on Friday, September 27, 2019.

Lay was accompanied by her hubby William Lay to mark the celebrations.

The two were on the verge of divorce back in 2015 but were able to sort out their differences and are still together.

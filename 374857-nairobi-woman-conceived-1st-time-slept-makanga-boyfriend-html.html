Nairobi woman narrates battling addiction, crime before getting saved

- Diana Naswa was brought up by a strict mother in the barracks and she always wanted to taste freedom

- When she met an older makanga at 14, the little girl fell in love and rolled in the sheets with him

- After their first try, she became pregnant and ran away from home

- The reformed woman shares her story after going through drug addiction, criminal activities to help others

At 14 years old, most girls are busy discovering makeup, playing dress up and catching up on the latest TV shows and movies.

But that was not the case for Nairobi lady Diana Naswa, her life at that age involved boys, concerts, truancy and more boys.

Speaking to Radio Jambo, the reformed woman said she became pregnant at that age after sleeping with her lover just once.

The beauty was smitten by a makanga who plied her route and would always board his matatu just to spend some time with him.

During those days, the easiest way to stay cool and relevant was to frequent CBD durig the day aboard a loud, pimped up matatu.

Lucky for her, Diana already had a free pass whenever she wanted to travel. Her encounters with the tout led her to his bed.

“At that age, I had already run away from home because I felt like my mum was too harsh whenever it came to punishing me. We were five kids but she always punished me thoroughly,” the lady said.

The woman explained that she ended up sleeping with the makanga because she craved for love and attention and he was the only one who could understand her.

She ended up leading a life of addiction, crime, prostitution before finally reforming and getting saved.

Kenyan man rides bike for 900km to raise KSh 4.4M to keep 600 students in school

- Ahmed Jibril embarked on a journey to raise at least KSh 3 million to sustain a feeding programme at Kakamega Township Primary School

- He, however, surpassed the target by KSh 1.4 million courtesy of support from well-wishers

- The money raised from the 900km bike riding marathon will be used to provide lunch for 635 pupils at the school for the entire 2021

- Jibril said the funds would go a long way in ensuring pupils get proper lunch over the next one year

A Nairobi based man has raised over KSh 4 million to feed and help keep 600 pupils in school after riding a bicycle for 900kms.

Ahmed Jibril embarked on a journey to raise at least KSh 3 million to sustain a feeding programme at Kakamega Township Primary School in June 2020 but surpassed the target by KSh 1.4 million courtesy of well-wishers' support.

The money raised from the 900km bike riding marathon dubbed Mama Ibado Charity (MIC) Bikeathon will be utilised to provide lunch for 635 pupils at the Kakamega based school for the entire 2021.

“We surpassed our target for the month-long exercise to raise KSh 4.4 million. This overwhelming support from our friends and the Kenyan community shows how much we value education for our children, especially the vulnerable,” said Jibril.

According to him, the funds would go a long way in ensuring pupils get proper lunch over the next one year.

He said this would boost their concentration in class as they recover from time lost during the COVID-19 outbreak which disrupted the economy and learning schedules across the country.

Kenyan schools re-opened on January 4, nine months after the outbreak of COVID-19 in March 2020.

Jibril disclosed the foundation has been providing food for 485 children in the school for the last two years but the enrollment number increased to 635 by the end of the 2019 academic year.

“To help students follow safety protocols after returning to school, we are planning to increase water points and equip them with soap to boost hygiene," the philathropist stated."We are also in the process of installing temperature screening points and donate reusable masks to the learners,” he added.

Nura Ali, the MIC programme development director, said the feeding programme has not only seen the pupils enjoy a healthy life but kept them in school.

“The school feeding programme has led to reduced absenteeism, especially for female children. The teachers have also noted improved cognitive functions amongst the students,” Ali said.

Since the introduction of the feeding programme, the school has recorded improved performance as indicated by KCPE results.

Overall, the KCPE mean score in 2019 rose to 271 compared to 265 in 2018.

Based on the 2019 KCPE results, two girls who are now in Lugulu Girls High School and one who is in Kamusinga Boys Hgh school were admitted to national schools.

The MIC which was formed in 2011 has also expanded from feeding 50 seniors in Isiolo to 650 seniors in Isiolo and Kakamega counties.

Kakamega senator Cleophas Malala has allegedly engaged in an ugly confrontation with Homa Bay woman representative Gladys Wanga at a tallying centre in Matungu constituency.

Trouble began when Malala, who is a member of the Amani Nation Congress (ANC) party was allegedly informed of voter bribery incidents at Bulimbo Primary School, forcing him to rush to the scene.

Malala descended on the woman rep with kicks and slaps in a physical altercation that is now synonymous with the honourable members.

Rigging claims

The ANC lawmaker had earlier claimed the ODM party was planning to rig the elections in the constituency.

He claimed the Opposition outfit was issuing unique masks to its members in a bid to rig the by-election.

"We have an issue with how ODM team are planning to interfere with this process. They are issuing unique masks that are going to identify their supporters. They have already compromised some presiding officers who will identify the supporters with the unique masks. In some instances, they will be given two ballots to cast," Malala said.

Echesa slaps IEBC official

Another violent incident was reported in the same constituency on Thursday, March 4, morning where a video showing former Sports Cabinet Secretary Rashid Echesa slapping an IEBC official emerged.

In the video, Echesa was heard telling the male official that he could not chase the United Democratic Alliance agent outside.

He then pulled down his face mask and slapped the poll official.

"Our agents are outside the polling station my friend. You cannot chase our agents outside. Why are you chasing them outside? Who ordered you?” Echesa posed angrily.

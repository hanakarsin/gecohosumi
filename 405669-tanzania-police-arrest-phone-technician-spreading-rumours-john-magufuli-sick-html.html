Tanzania: Police arrest phone technician for spreading rumours John Magufuli is sick

- Police said he suspect identified as Charles Majura would face charges relating to computer misuse and cybercrime

- Kinondoni regional police commander Ramadhani Kingai said Majura published unverified information suggesting Magufuli has been sick

- Magufuli has not appeared in public for a couple of weeks with his office remaining tight-lipped about his whereabouts

Tanzania authorities are holding a 35-year-old phone repairer on allegations of spreading fake news about President John Pombe Magufuli.

Kinondoni regional police commander Ramadhani Kingai said the suspect identified as Charles Majura would face charges relating to computer misuse and cybercrime.

According to Millard Ayo News, Majura had published unverified information suggesting Magufuli has been sick.

The police boss warned the public to desist from circulating misinformation adding that the law enforcement agencies would not to stop to initiate arrest of such individuals.

Ramadhan was addressing the media during a workshop that brought together all police provincial commanders on Saturday, March 13.

Magufuli's absence

Magufuli has not appeared in public for a couple of weeks with his office remaining tight-lipped about his whereabouts even as speculation about his health continue to take shape and worry Tanzanians.

On Thursday, March 11, the government denied reports that the CCM leader was sick and called out those circulating the misleading information.

Opposition leader Tundu Lissu was among the first Tanzanians to raise alarm over Magufuli's health.

He alleged the Tanzanian leader was flown to Kenya to seek treatment after falling ill.

However, Tanzanian prime minister Kassim Majaliwa dismissed the reports saying the president was only overwhelmed with work and not health issues.

"The president has loads of files to deal with, now when he stays there (in office) going through them you say he is ill...The president has his own work schedule and can’t appear under your directions," he told BBC Africa.

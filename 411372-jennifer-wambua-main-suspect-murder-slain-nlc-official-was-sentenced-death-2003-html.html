Jennifer Wambua: Main Suspect in Murder of Slain NLC Official Was Sentenced to Death in 2003

- Forensic analysis placed Peter Mwangi Njenga at the scene of Jennifer Wambua's murder

- Wambua had an encounter with the main suspect who offered her drinking water as she returned from a private prayer in Ngong Forest

- The criminal data system confirmed Njenga is a serial rapist who sexually abused his victims before killing them

Detectives probing the mysterious murder of the National Land Commission assistant director of communications, Jennifer Wambua, have made a breakthrough in the ongoing investigations.

The homicide team from the Crime Research and Intelligence Bureau, Cyber Crime, Scenes of Crime and Special Service Unit forensically placed Peter Mwangi Njenga at the murder scene.

Njenga, also known as Ole Sankale, was the last person in the company of the communications expert on the day she was last seen around Ngong Forest.

Wambua visited the forest on Friday, March 12, for private prayers over her depression on the advice of a Machakos-based priest with whom she constantly communicated.

On coming from the prayers, Wambua had an encounter with the main suspect who offered her drinking water.

It was at that point that detectives believe Njenga lured, sexually assaulted and killed the former Machakos-based Kenya News Agency journalist.

Who is Njenga?

The suspect has several previous criminal records of similar nature.

The criminal data system confirmed Njenga is a serial rapist who sexually abused his victims before killing them after violently robbing them.

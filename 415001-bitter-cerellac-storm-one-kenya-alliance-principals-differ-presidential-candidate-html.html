Bitter Cerellac: Storm in One Kenya Alliance as Principals Differ on Presidential Candidate

- Some members of the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) have supposed Gideon Moi may ditch the coalition after the nullification of BBI

- They claim the Baringo senator could be headed to a coalition with ODM leader Raila Odinga

- OKA united the leaders, Kalonzo Musyoka, Gideon Moi, and Moses Wetangula, who had looked forward to benefitting from positions created by BBI

There are reported wrangles in the One Kenya Alliance (OKA), whose leaders are divided on whom to vie for the presidency in next year's General Election.

Since the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) nullification, the OKA luminaries are said to have found themselves in disarray, despite the constitutional change initiative having united them.

They had looked forward to benefitting from the additional executive positions that the initiative sought to create.

According to the Kenya African National Union (KANU) party, the nullification of the BBI process was set to tear apart the coalition as they had come together to promote the proposed constitutional amendments.

"In one way or another, we all support the BBI. This is because without the BBI then even this OKA coalition will not exist," Nick Salat, KANU secretary-general, told Taifa Leo.

After the setback suffered by BBI, leaders in OKA claim Baringo senator Gideon Moi could be planning to ditch the coalition and move to support ODM leader Raila Odinga as the next year's general polls draw closer.

Salat defended the Baringo senator on his friendship with Raila saying the two are long-time friends.

"Raila and Moi are friends and we can visit him (Raila) whenever we have the chance," said Salat.

Gideon has lately made regular visits to Raila's home, his colleagues in OKA suspecting there could be a formation in the cooking.

The senator united with Raila at his home in Karen in April, also visiting the former prime minister at his residence in Kisumu.

Lugari MP Ayub Savula began the speculation about a possible Raila-Moi union ahead of the 2022 polls, the legislator insisting that the ANC party leader will be the one to fly the OKA flag during the polls.

Savula claimed that Ford-Kenya chief Moses Wetang’ula had already agreed to support, Mudavadi in 2022, putting high the chances of Mudavadi to be OKA's presidential candidate.

According to Savula. Gideon's walkout would be inconsequential.

"We know that the OKA candidate is Mudavadi. Moi has no influence over the Rift Valley. BBI was shot down in his own backyard. Kalonzo is also not a strong candidate and since Wetang'ula supports Mudavadi, Wiper cannot fly OKA's flag," said Savula."Gideon joined politics after Mudavadi, so he has no chance of winning the OKA ticket. We have no other candidate but Mudavadi, even if we are called extremists, ”added the MP.

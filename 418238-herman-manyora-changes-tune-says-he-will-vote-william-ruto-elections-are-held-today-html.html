Herman Manyora Declares He Would Vote for William Ruto if Elections Were Held Today

  • Herman Manyora has been on the record saying Deputy President William Ruto has no chance of winning the 2022 presidency
  • In February, the analyst predicted ODM leader Raila Odinga would be a transitional president in 2022 before paving way for KANU's Gideon Moi in 2027
  • He, however, argued the incessant attacks on the DP made him change his tune

Renowned political analyst Herman Manyora who has been on the record saying Deputy President William Ruto has no chance of winning the 2022 presidency has changed tune.

The current affairs commentator said he would vote for the DP if elections were held today.

Speaking to TV producer Jadiel Kabiro in a video posted on YouTube, Manyora said his vote for Ruto would be out of sympathy following incessant attacks against him by former prime minister Raila Odinga and a section of pro-handshake supporters.

"Somebody was telling me I have never supported Ruto but the way he is being attacked now, I am now going to support him. If elections were held today I would vote for him," Manyora said.

Unfair attacks

The University of Nairobi don said Ruto was being attacked unfairly and corruption allegations labelled against him without his side of the story being heard. He claimed Kenya is a country of thieves.

"It doesn't help. This is a country of thieves. If you call someone a thief, how does it help you?" Manyora posed.

In another analysis on his YouTube channel, Manyora asked Raila to leave Ruto alone saying his risks losing votes if he continues to attack the Jubilee deputy party leader instead of championing his 2022 agenda.

"If somebody is listening, tell Raila Odinga to leave Ruto alone. Don't call Ruto a thief because all Kenyans' are thieves. So you are gaining nothing from it. In fact when you call him a thief, they say he is our thief," he said.

Manyora's previous stance

In April, Manyora had claimed Ruto was losing political relevance owing to the humiliations treated to his camp by President Uhuru Kenyatta and his team.

He likened Ruto's way of politicking to Usain Bolts sprints, insinuating the DP's kind of politics does not have the capacity of standing the test of time.

The analyst had foreseen the DP becoming a deserted man by 2022 after some instances where his foot soldiers walked out on him.

In February, the analyst had predicted Raila would be a transitional president before paving way for Kanu's Gideon Moi.

"Gideon Moi is a prince. A prince does not fight for the crown. The prince is given the crown...sometimes through inheritance. Gideon may be president, not through fighting but by fitting into a succession plan."

A former General Service Unit (GSU) officer has been implicated in the Matuu Equity Bank robbery incident that left the police helpless.

The suspect who was fired from the service in Mombasa was believed to have commanded the five-man gang that stole an unknown amount of money.

Boda boda rider arrested

According to Daily Nation, a multi-agency team formed to track the burglars was not successful in nabbing them.

A boda boda rider identified as Sylvester Kilonzo, who reportedly ferried two gang members from Matuu to Kimangu market, was arrested.

Kilonzo, who is in police custody, would help detectives led by Machakos county police commander Issa Mohammed to trace the gang members.

Equity Bank confirmed the incident in a statement through its Twitter handle on Tuesday, July 27.

Bank robbers posed as customers

According to a police report, the gunmen raided the financial facility on the morning of Tuesday, July 27, while posing as customers.

The suspects first grabbed an AK47 rifle from a police officer manning the entrance at the bank before they made their way into the baking hall, firing into the air.

Yatta police commander Mary Njoki said the robbers later on engaged other officers who came in to help in a gunfight for about 15 minutes before escaping.

“For about 15 minutes there was heavy gunfight between our officers and the thieves who later managed to escape. One of the thieves was shot but managed to escape,” Njoki stated.

Failed bank heist

Earlier in July, a planned bank heist in Nairobi downtown aborted at the last minute after a team of detectives got wind of the robbers' intention.

Charles Mulo, Fredrick Muderwa, Jesse Muriuki, Gabriel Mungai and Reuben Njuka had booked themselves into guest rooms in the same building, which shares a wall with Prime Bank OTC branch.

According to the DCI, who alerted colleagues from Kamukunji police station, the gang was about to break into the bank when they were arrested in the wee hours of Friday, July 2.

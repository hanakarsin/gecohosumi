Uhuru Nominates Four Candidates to Fill Vacant IEBC Commissioners' Positions

  • President Uhuru Kenyatta forwarded the names of Juliana Wihonge Chirera, Francis Mathenge Wanderi, Irene Cherop Masit and Justice Abonyo Nyangaya to National Assembly for vetting
  • Uhuru received the names of the four candidates from the Elizabeth Muli-led selection panel which interviewed 36 candidates
  • If appointed, they will replace Roselyn Akombe, Margaret Mwachanya, Paul Kurgat and former vice-chair Consolata Maina who resigned from the IEBC

President Uhuru Kenyatta has nominated four candidates to fill vacant positions in the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC).

The head of state nominated Juliana Wihonge Chirera, Francis Mathenge Wanderi, Irene Cherop Masit and Justice Abonyo Nyangaya on Thursday, August 5, days after the selection panel forwarded the names to him.

Uhuru forwarded the names of the quartet to the National Assembly for vetting. Speaker Justin Muturi will forward the names to the Justice and Legal Affairs Committee which is expected to interview the nominees.

The committee will then forward a report to the Parliament for debate.

The Elizabeth Muli-led selection panel forward the names of the four to Uhuru earlier this week after choosing them from the 36 Kenyans who were interviewed.

If appointed, they will replace Roselyn Akombe, Margaret Mwachanya, Paul Kurgat and former vice-chair Consolata Maina who resigned from the commission.

The commissioners resigned from the electoral body, citing a lack of confidence in the leadership of its chairperson, Wafula Chebukati.

Akombe left the commission days before the repeat of the 2017 presidential election.

Over 700 Kenyans had sent in applications for the vacant posts of the four commissioners with 120 of the candidates being holders of doctorate degrees (PhD).

Some of the top names who had made into the final list were Environment Cabinet Secretary Keriako Tobiko's sister Naisiae Tobiko, Kenya’s ambassador to Zimbabwe Lucy Jebet Chelimo, and Kenya’s deputy permanent representative to the United Nations Koki Muli.

Others who were shortlisted included Salim Ndemo (former commissioner at the Public Service), Florence Jaoko and Kagwiria Mbogori (ex-Kenya National Commission on Human Rights chairpersons) and former Gwasi lawmaker Felix Nyauchi.
